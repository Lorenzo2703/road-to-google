# 🧡 Googleyness & Leadership Questions

> The most likely Google behavioral questions about Googleyness and Leadership.

---

## What is Googleyness?

Googleyness evaluates whether you align with Google's culture and values:
- **Doing the right thing**: Ethical decisions, user focus, integrity
- **Working collaboratively**: No ego, inclusive, supportive
- **Thriving in ambiguity**: Comfortable with uncertainty, proactive
- **Valuing feedback**: Growth mindset, coachable, self-aware
- **Bringing joy**: Passion, curiosity, positive energy

---

## Top 15 Googleyness Questions

### 1. Tell me about a time you did the right thing, even when it was hard.
**What they want**: Ethical courage, integrity, user-first thinking

### 2. Describe a situation where you had to work with someone difficult.
**What they want**: Empathy, patience, constructive resolution

### 3. How do you handle disagreements on your team?
**What they want**: Collaborative conflict resolution, data-driven decisions

### 4. Tell me about a time you helped someone outside your direct responsibilities.
**What they want**: Selflessness, community mindset, going above and beyond

### 5. Describe a time you navigated a highly ambiguous situation.
**What they want**: Comfort with uncertainty, ability to create structure

### 6. How do you approach working with people from different backgrounds?
**What they want**: Inclusivity, cultural awareness, respect for diversity

### 7. Tell me about a time you received feedback that changed how you work.
**What they want**: Coachability, growth mindset, self-awareness

### 8. Describe a time you had to balance speed with quality.
**What they want**: Pragmatism, good judgment, understanding trade-offs

### 9. What do you do when you disagree with a decision your team has made?
**What they want**: Disagree-and-commit, constructive challenge, team player

### 10. Tell me about a passion project or something you do outside of work.
**What they want**: Curiosity, intrinsic motivation, well-roundedness

### 11. How do you keep learning and growing as an engineer?
**What they want**: Continuous learning, intellectual curiosity

### 12. Describe a time you made a mistake that affected others.
**What they want**: Accountability, honesty, recovery skills

### 13. How would you improve a process that's "good enough"?
**What they want**: Continuous improvement, not settling for mediocrity

### 14. Tell me about a time you advocated for something unpopular.
**What they want**: Courage of convictions, data-driven advocacy

### 15. Why Google? Why now? Why this role?
**What they want**: Genuine enthusiasm, specific knowledge of the role, clear motivation

## Top 10 Leadership Questions (for L4)

### 1. Tell me about a time you led a project or initiative.
**Note**: L4 leadership is about influence, not authority

### 2. How do you handle a situation where your team is behind schedule?
**What they want**: Pragmatic prioritization, communication, ownership

### 3. Describe a time you made a technical decision that impacted the team.
**What they want**: Decision-making process, trade-off analysis

### 4. How do you onboard new teammates?
**What they want**: Mentoring ability, empathy for newcomers

### 5. Tell me about a time you pushed back on a requirement.
**What they want**: Good judgment, communication skills, user focus

### 6. How do you ensure code quality across your team?
**What they want**: Code review culture, standards, leading by example

### 7. Describe a time you improved a team process.
**What they want**: Process improvement, initiative, measurable impact

### 8. How do you handle competing priorities?
**What they want**: Prioritization framework, stakeholder management

### 9. Tell me about a time you helped a struggling teammate.
**What they want**: Empathy, mentoring, psychological safety

### 10. How do you balance technical debt with feature development?
**What they want**: Long-term thinking, pragmatism, communication
